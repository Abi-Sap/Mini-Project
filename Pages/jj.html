Jerry John Rawlings

Homepage Kwame Nkrumah John Agyekum Kufuor John Jerry Rawlings

About

Jerry John Rawlings was a Ghanaian military officer, aviator and politician who led the country for a brief period in 1979, and then from 1981 to 2001. He led a military junta until 1992, and then served two terms as the democratically elected president of Ghana.

Born: June 22, 1947, Accra.

Died: November 12, 2020, Korle Bu Teaching Hospital, Accra

Children: Zanetor Agyeman-Rawlings, Kimathi Rawlings, Amina Rawlings, Yaa Asantewaa Rawlings

Spouse: Nana Konadu Agyeman-Rawlings (m. 1977–2020)

Parents: Victoria Agbotui, James Ramsey John

Siblings: Mr. Isaac Newton Nii Lante Addy

Presidential term: January 7, 1993 – January 7, 2001

Rank:He earned the rank of flight lieutenant in April 1978

Achievements

  1. Achieved political and economic stability in a region rife with conflicts (for example Liberia, Côte d’Ivoire, Togo, Guinea Bissau and Sierra Leone).

  2. Created 110 districts through non-partisan district level elections. Education, infrastructure developments and healthcare all devolved to the district level. Annual government subvention by law goes to the district — unthinkable in many other African countries.

  3. Made sure that every district had at least two senior secondary schools.)

  4. Built first-ever memorials to Kwame Nkrumah and W.E.B. DuBois.

  5. Followed an independent foreign policy

  6. Started the Expansion Project and the Renovation of the 37 Military Hospital.

  7. Established the University for Development Studies, and used his Hunger Award Prize Money to buy books for the University’s Library.

  8. Potable water was provided for so many communities that at the time he was leaving office, guinea worm infestation had virtually been eradicated from Ghana.

  9. There was the smooth, historic transfer of power from the NDC to the NPP.

  10. Expanded electricity to Northern parts of Ghana – hitherto ignored by ruling elites since 1957.

  11. Ghana’s huge reputation in international peacekeeping, coupled with the ability of H. E Jerry John Rawlings to get the illustrious son of Ghana, Kofi Annan, elected as the Secretary-General of the United Nations.

  12. The creation of District Assemblies’ Common Fund, the GetFund, the Road Fund, the EDIF and the Energy Fund

  13. Passed the Ghana Educational Trust Fund (GET Fund) that is today educating millions of Ghanaians.
